# Test User Interviews — Onboarding Experience
# 5 participants | June 2025 | B2B SaaS Product

---

## Interview 1
**Participant:** James, Head of Operations, 50-person logistics company
**Tenure:** New customer, signed up 3 weeks ago
**Interviewer:** Ali

**Q: Walk me through your onboarding experience so far.**
"Honestly it's been painful. We signed the contract, got a welcome email with a PDF guide, and then... nothing. I had to figure everything out myself. The PDF was 47 pages long. Nobody reads a 47-page PDF."

**Q: What was the hardest part?**
"Getting my team set up. I couldn't figure out how to invite users with different permission levels. I spent two hours on it before giving up and emailing support. They replied the next day. That's a 24-hour delay just to add a team member."

**Q: Did you consider leaving?**
"Yes. My manager asked me after week one if it was worth it. I didn't have a good answer yet."

**Q: What would have made it easier?**
"A live person or a chatbot walking me through the first setup. Not a PDF. And clear tooltips on every screen — tell me what to click next. I shouldn't have to guess."

## Interview 2
**Participant:** Priya, Product Manager, 200-person fintech startup
**Tenure:** Customer for 6 weeks, completed onboarding at week 4
**Interviewer:** Ali

**Q: You took 4 weeks to fully onboard — what caused the delay?**
"Two things. First, data migration. We had existing data in another tool and there was no import wizard. We had to manually enter everything or write a custom CSV. That took our engineer 3 days. Second, I didn't know what 'done' looked like. There was no checklist saying 'you're ready to go live.'"

**Q: What was your emotional state during onboarding?**
"Frustrated for the first two weeks. Relieved when it finally clicked. But those two weeks were genuinely stressful — I had promised my team we'd be up and running in a week."

**Q: What was the turning point?**
"A customer success manager finally scheduled a 30-minute call with us. She showed us three things in the product that we had completely missed. After that call, we were productive within a day. That one call did more than all the documentation combined."

**Q: What would you change?**
"That call should happen on day one, not week three. Or at minimum, there should be an interactive tour that shows those same three things automatically."

## Interview 3
**Participant:** Tom, IT Manager, 80-person professional services firm
**Tenure:** Evaluated product, did not convert — churned during trial
**Interviewer:** Ali

**Q: You didn't end up purchasing — can you tell us why?**
"We ran out of time in the trial before we saw value. 14-day trial, but it took us 5 days just to get the integration with our existing systems working. By the time that was done we had 9 days left, and honestly we hadn't even started testing the core features."

**Q: Was support available during the trial?**
"There was a help centre. It was fine for basic questions but our integration questions weren't covered. I submitted a support ticket on day 3 and heard back on day 6. That's 3 days gone."

**Q: What would have made you stay?**
"A sandbox environment with pre-loaded data so I could see the product working immediately without setting anything up. I just wanted to see if it could do what we needed before investing time in setup."

**Q: Any other feedback?**
"The error messages during integration setup were useless. 'Connection failed' — okay, why? What do I do about it? I'm not a developer, I need plain English."

## Interview 4
**Participant:** Maria, VP of Sales, 120-person e-commerce company
**Tenure:** Customer for 3 months, onboarded in 6 days
**Interviewer:** Ali

**Q: You onboarded relatively quickly — what went well?**
"We had a dedicated implementation partner assigned to us from day one. She was incredible. She knew exactly what we needed and configured everything for us. We just showed up and it was ready."

**Q: Do you think all customers get that experience?**
"Probably not — we're on the enterprise plan. I've heard from a friend at a smaller company who had a terrible time."

**Q: What could be improved even for your experience?**
"The training materials for my sales team. I had to create my own training slides because the official ones were too technical. My team are salespeople, not IT people. The product is great but the training assumes too much technical knowledge."

**Q: One thing you'd fix?**
"Role-specific onboarding tracks. The setup for an admin is completely different from what a salesperson needs to know. Don't give everyone the same experience."

## Interview 5
**Participant:** David, Founder, 12-person design agency
**Tenure:** Customer for 2 months, onboarding took 10 days
**Interviewer:** Ali

**Q: What was your first week like?**
"Chaotic. I was setting it up while also running client projects. I'd log in, get confused, close the tab, come back two days later. The product didn't pull me back in — there were no reminders, no 'hey, you haven't finished setting up' emails."

**Q: What finally got you over the line?**
"I watched a YouTube tutorial — not from your company, from a random user. It was 8 minutes long and explained everything clearly. Why isn't that the first thing I see when I sign up?"

**Q: What features do you use most now?**
"Client collaboration and time tracking. But I didn't even know time tracking existed until week 6. It was buried in a submenu. I would have used it from day one if I'd known."

**Q: What would you tell the product team?**
"Feature discoverability is zero. I'm still probably missing things. Send me a monthly email saying 'here are 3 features you haven't tried yet.' I'd actually read that."
